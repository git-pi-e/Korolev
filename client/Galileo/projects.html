Projects

Pyro Board

Using the concept of standing waves in a Ruben's Tube - a large tube sealed at both ends, one of which is connected to a speaker. The tube has many tiny holes, and when standing waves are created in the ignited propane pumped into the tube, there is variation in pressure and we see the same waves in the flames. The Pyro Board extends this concept to 2 dimensions.

Musical Tesla Coil - Zeusaphone

The Zeusaphone is a variety of solid state tesla coil that has been modified to produce musical tones by modulating its spark output. The musical tone results directly from the passage of the spark through the air.

3D-LiDAR

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) is a concept closely related to RADAR and SONAR, and works by illuminating targets using light. It measures the distance to a target by measuring the reflected light with a sensor, and is able to detect most of the features as seen by the camera along with the exact distance of where that point is.

Acoustic Beamforming

Using the concept of Microphone Arrays and Mircophone Steering to focus towards a given direction/source of sound while also eliminating all ambient sounds and noise, Acoustic Beamforming aims to personalize sound itself.

Star Tracker

Building a computerized telescope Star Tracker, which would be configured to Stellarium, a sky-viewing software. This will help in easily spotting low-intensity objects in the sky.

Microbial Fuel Cell

Microbial Fuel Cells are devices that use bacteria as the catalysts to oxidize organic and inorganic matter and generate current. Thusly they can be used for detecting bacteria, and signs of life, possibly even on other planets.

CanSat

A CanSat is a type of sounding rocket payload used to teach space technology. In CanSat competitions, the payload is required to fit inside the volume of a typical soda can, and have a mass below 350g. We're building a telemetry CanSat to collect and transmit data from the flight and weather conditions in real time.

Comet Dust Trails Simulation

A Comet's dust particles can vary in size anywhere between a few microns long to a few cm long, and when the comet trails containing these particles intersect the orbits of a planet they can create storms, and these meteor impacts can inflict serious damage to spacecrafts in its path. However, using precise models we can predict the path these dust particles follow - hence avoid collisions! This is exactly what we're trying to do in this project.
